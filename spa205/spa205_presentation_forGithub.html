Cultural remedies & beliefs in healthcare

  • Natural Medicine
  • Alternative Medicine
  • Traditional Remedies

These practices are used instead of standard treatments and are not generally recognized by the medical community as standard or conventional medical approaches.

Examples include: Herbs, Homeopathy, Iridology, Reflexology, Acupuncture, and Sobados.

Homeopathy

Focuses on bringing the entire body back into homeostasis, or balance.

Homeopathic medicine uses very minute doses of medicine to stimulate the body's own defenses and the healing process. These doses are so small that in Western allopathic medicine they are often viewed as ineffective.

Iridology

The study of changes in the eyes. It focuses on changes in the colored part of the eye (the iris).

It identifies tissue changes and degenerative signs in the body which are reflected in signs and discolorations in the eyes.

Reflexology

The science which deals with the thought that there are reflex areas in the feet and hands that correspond to all of the glands, organs and parts of the body.

Acupuncture

The technique of inserting thin needles through the skin at specific points on the body to control pain and other symptoms.

Sobados

Traditional massages are done by a sobador. These sobadores are people who have learned the therapy from their ancestors and who have a "good hand."

Mal de Ojo (The Evil Eye)

The belief that by staring or gazing at a person or another's property, harm can be projected.

Preventative Amulets: La mano negra, Ojo de venado, and Middle Eastern amulets. Most are worn with a red ribbon or cord.

Empacho

A folk illness where soft or difficult-to-digest food becomes lodged in the stomach. Common in infants.

Symptoms:
  • anorexia, stomachache
  • vomiting, pain with diarrhea
  • generalized abdominal fullness
Causes:
  • eating too much or spoiled food
  • eating at wrong time of day
  • changing formulas rapidly

Mollera caída (Fallen Fontanelle)

The mollera is thought to sink in when an infant is pulled from the breast too quickly, leading to fussiness and diarrhea.

Folk remedies: Sucking on the fontanelle, pushing up on the soft palate, or hanging the child upside down while tapping their feet.

Susto

"Fright" caused by a scary event where the soul leaves the body. Symptoms: insomnia, nightmares, fevers.

Rituals:
  • Applying holy water/oil to forehead.
  • Passing an egg over the body in circular motions.
  • Barrida - sweeping a bundle of herbs over the body.

Health Maintenance Beliefs

  • Protection: Wear proper clothing (socks, hats) to protect from the sereno of the night.
  • Temperature: Beware of sudden changes (e.g., don't shower after ironing).
  • Diet: Certain foods should not be mixed or eaten at night.
  • Hot & Cold Theory: Treat hot illnesses with cold remedies, and vice versa.
